# Default VC Evaluation Criteria

When sector-specific investment criteria are not available through research, use these defaults adapted to the startup's sector and stage.

## Core Criteria

### 1. Team (Weight: 40%)

The team is the single most important factor at early stages. VCs bet on people first.

- **Technical founder** — Is there a builder on the founding team, or is this outsourced?
- **Domain expertise** — Do these founders deeply understand the problem space?
- **Founder-market fit** — Why are THESE people uniquely positioned to solve THIS problem?
- **Previous experience** — Prior startups, exits, or relevant industry roles
- **Coachability** — Open to feedback? Willing to pivot if data says so?
- **Recruiting ability** — Can they attract top talent?
- **Grit signals** — How long have they been at this? What have they sacrificed?
- Score range: 1-10

### 2. Market (Weight: 25%)

The market must be large enough to support venture-scale returns.

- **TAM / SAM / SOM** — Total, serviceable, and obtainable market with credible bottom-up sizing
- **Growth rate** — Is this market expanding? At what rate?
- **Timing** — Why now? What inflection point makes this possible today?
- **Market structure** — Winner-take-all vs fragmented? Platform vs point solution?
- **Regulatory environment** — Tailwinds or headwinds?
- **Customer willingness to pay** — Is there a proven budget for this?
- Score range: 1-10

### 3. Product (Weight: 15%)

Evidence that the team can build and that users want what they're building.

- **Product-market fit signals** — Retention, engagement, NPS, organic growth
- **Technical differentiation** — Is this hard to replicate? Does it use proprietary technology?
- **Demo quality** — Does it actually work? Is it impressive?
- **Design sense** — Is there craftsmanship? Does the team care about UX?
- **Platform potential** — Can this expand beyond the initial use case?
- Score range: 1-10

### 4. Traction (Weight: 10%)

Evidence of real-world validation. Stage-dependent expectations.

- **Revenue** — ARR, MRR, or GMV depending on the model
- **Users** — Active users, growth rate, retention curves
- **Growth rate** — Month-over-month or quarter-over-quarter
- **Retention** — Cohort analysis, churn rate, expansion revenue
- **Pipeline** — LOIs, pilot agreements, waitlist size
- Score range: 1-10

### 5. Moat (Weight: 10%)

What prevents a well-funded competitor from copying this in 6 months?

- **Network effects** — Does the product get better with more users?
- **Data advantage** — Is proprietary data being accumulated?
- **Switching costs** — How painful is it to leave once adopted?
- **IP / Proprietary tech** — Patents, unique algorithms, hard-to-replicate systems
- **Speed of execution** — Is the team moving so fast that catching up is impractical?
- **Regulatory moat** — Licenses, certifications, compliance that take time
- Score range: 1-10

## Stage-Specific Adjustments

### Pre-Seed

| Criterion | Weight | Expectation |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Team | 50% | Strong founder-market fit, technical capability |
| Market | 25% | Large and growing, clear "why now" |
| Product | 15% | Prototype or MVP, doesn't need to be polished |
| Traction | 5% | Nice-to-have, not required |
| Moat | 5% | Thesis-level, not yet proven |

### Seed

| Criterion | Weight | Expectation |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Team | 40% | Full founding team, complementary skills |
| Market | 25% | Validated market demand, clear ICP |
| Product | 15% | Working product, early users giving feedback |
| Traction | 10% | Some revenue or strong user growth |
| Moat | 10% | Early defensibility signals |

### Series A

| Criterion | Weight | Expectation |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Team | 30% | Leadership team in place, key hires made |
| Market | 25% | Proven demand, clear GTM, expanding TAM |
| Product | 15% | Product-market fit established |
| Traction | 20% | $1-3M ARR (SaaS), strong growth rate |
| Moat | 10% | Emerging defensibility |

### Series B

| Criterion | Weight | Expectation |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Team | 20% | Scaled leadership, VP-level hires |
| Market | 20% | Market leadership position or clear path |
| Product | 15% | Multi-product potential, platform evolution |
| Traction | 25% | $5-15M ARR, proven unit economics |
| Moat | 20% | Clear competitive advantage |

## Sector-Specific Considerations

### SaaS / B2B Software
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) > 120% is gold standard
- CAC payback < 18 months
- Gross margins > 70%
- Logo churn < 5% annually
- Land-and-expand motion

### Consumer / B2C
- Viral coefficient and organic growth
- Engagement metrics (DAU/MAU ratio)
- Retention curves (week 1, week 4, week 12)
- Monetization without killing growth
- Community and brand

### Fintech
- Regulatory strategy and compliance
- Unit economics at scale
- Partnership with incumbents
- Trust and security posture
- Revenue model sustainability

### AI / ML
- Proprietary data or model advantage
- Not just an API wrapper — what's the moat?
- Inference cost trajectory
- Accuracy vs cost tradeoffs
- Workflow integration depth

### Marketplace / Platform
- Supply-demand balance
- Liquidity metrics
- Take rate justification
- Chicken-and-egg strategy
- Disintermediation risk

### Hardware / Deep Tech
- Manufacturing scalability
- Bill of materials trajectory
- Patent portfolio
- Regulatory pathway
- Capital intensity vs returns timeline

## Scoring Calibration Guide

| Score | Meaning | VC Context |
|-------|---------|------------|
| 1-2 | Poor | Fundamental problems, would not take the meeting |
| 3-4 | Below Average | Significant gaps, likely a pass |
| 5-6 | Average | Decent but nothing compelling, "interesting but..." |
| 7 | Good | Strong in this dimension, would continue diligence |
| 8 | Very Good | Impressive, stands out from deal flow |
| 9 | Excellent | Exceptional, top-decile quality |
| 10 | World-Class | Best-in-class, rarely seen, anchor for conviction |

Note: The median startup that approaches a VC scores around 4-5 on most criteria. A score of 7+ is genuinely investable in that dimension. Scores of 9-10 should be extremely rare — these are the criteria that make an investor say "I MUST be in this deal."
